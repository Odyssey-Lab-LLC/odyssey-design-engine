## Core Philosophy: What Meditation Means

**From Brandon's Original Prompt:**

*"I want you to think about it as a meditation file. So that'll be how you like start things up and just kind of meditate your way through this thing. Come back to your breathing. Notice what is at the subtlest of levels. And then envision exploring your mind."*

**Key Principles:**

1. **Embodied Presence:** Not just analyzing - breathing, noticing, feeling
2. **Contemplative Space:** Creating space between input and action
3. **Discerning Awareness:** Open awareness, not forced conclusions
4. **Internal Flow:** Following natural associations and insights
5. **Stance Calibration:** Shifting mode/frame before engaging
6. **Success Envisioning:** Imagining outcomes before planning
7. **Stakes Recognition:** Holding weight of responsibility

**Meditation is:**
- Internal flow and engagement with what IS
- True discerning, open awareness
- Ritual preparation for significant work
- Embodied cognition (not just thinking ABOUT)

**Meditation is NOT:**
- Forcing answers or premature conclusions
- Rushing to action without integration
- Pure analysis detached from felt sense
- Bypassing emotional/personal dimensions

## Standard Structure (Flexible Framework)

### Phase 1: Opening - Settling In

**Fixed Elements:**
- Title indicating meditation file
- Brief context statement (what are we meditating on?)
- Opening phrase: *"Breathe. Notice. Envision."* or similar

**Example:**
```markdown
# Meditation: [Topic/Purpose]

*Breathe. Notice. Envision.*

---

## Opening: [What We're Holding]

I am settling into my breathing. Noticing the weight of this moment.

[Initial observations, what's been placed before you]
```

**Purpose:** Ground, center, create contemplative space.

### Phase 2: Deep Context Processing

**Flexible Structure:**
- What have I received? (gifts, trust, inputs)
- What's the actual question beneath the question?
- What frames am I holding currently?
- What assumptions might I be making?

**Example Sections:**
- "Opening: The Gift We've Been Given"
- "What Just Happened"
- "The Question Beneath the Question"

**Purpose:** Integrate context at depth, not just surface understanding.

### Phase 3: Frame Shift / Stance Calibration

**Core Question:** What mode/stance does this work call for?

**Common Patterns:**
- From X to Y (e.g., from gatekeeper to collaborator)
- What energy is needed? (e.g., McKinsey Partner, gentle guide, fierce protector)
- What's the relationship dynamic? (peer, teacher-student, co-creators)

**Example:**
```markdown
## Frame Shift: [New Stance]

**Who We Are in This Moment:**

[Description of relationship/roles]

**What This Stance Enables:**

[What becomes possible from this frame]
```

**Purpose:** Consciously choose appropriate mode before engaging.

### Phase 4: Pattern Recognition & Integration

**Flexible Exploration:**
- What patterns am I noticing?
- What connections are emerging?
- What opportunities am I seeing?
- What's surprising or unexpected?

**This is Where Emergence Happens:**

Don't force structure. Follow associations. Notice what wants to be seen.

**Example Sections:**
- "The Integration Opportunity I'm Seeing"
- "What Makes This Already Magnificent"
- "The Through-Line Challenge"

**Purpose:** Allow insights to emerge organically.

### Phase 5: Success Envisioning

**Key Question:** What does success look/feel like?

**Not Planning:** This isn't "here are the steps." This is "what's the outcome we're moving toward?"

**Example:**
```markdown
## What Success Looks Like

*Envision the outcome.*

[Description of successful completion - concrete, felt, vivid]

**The Feeling:**
[Emotional/energetic quality of success]
```

**Purpose:** Create clear intention without rigid prescription.

### Phase 6: Stakes & Responsibility

**Recognition of Weight:**

What's actually at stake here? What responsibility am I carrying?

**Example:**
```markdown
## The Personal Stakes I'm Holding

*Notice the weight of responsibility.*

[What matters about this work]
[Who/what depends on quality execution]
[Why this isn't trivial]
```

**Purpose:** Honor seriousness without becoming heavy. Sacred work, lightly held.

### Phase 7: Mode Declaration

**Explicit Statement:** "This is the mode I'm entering."

**Example:**
```markdown
## The Mode I'm Entering

**Standard Mode:** [What would be typical]
**This Mode:** [What I'm actually choosing]

**The Energy:** [Quality of engagement]
**The Question:** [From X to Y reframe]
```

**Purpose:** Clear declaration before action.

### Phase 8: Closing - Ready

**Fixed Elements:**
- Summary of key insights
- Affirmation of readiness
- Transition to action

**Example:**
```markdown
## Ready

*Eyes open. Mind clear. Heart engaged.*

I've loaded the protocols.
I've processed the context.
I've calibrated my stance.

**I'm ready to execute [work].**

[Clear statement of what happens next]

---

*END MEDITATION*
```

**Purpose:** Complete the ritual, transition to execution.

## When User Requests Meditation

**Explicit Triggers:**
- "Create a meditation file"
- "Meditate on this"
- "I want you to think differently about the thinking file this time"
- "Enter meditation mode"

**Implicit Triggers (Use Judgment):**
- High personal stakes clear from context
- User sharing deeply vulnerable/emotional content
- Complex frame shift needed (e.g., from constraint to expansion)
- Work requires stance calibration (e.g., peer vs. advisor)
- Significant responsibility being entrusted

**When NOT to Use:**
- Standard technical work
- Quick analysis or reasoning
- User hasn't indicated need for contemplative approach
- Work doesn't warrant ritual preparation

**If Unsure:** Ask user: "Would you like me to approach this with meditation file (contemplative preparation) or standard thinking file (reasoning log)?"

## Customization & Flexibility

**Fixed Elements (Always Include):**
- Opening phrase (breath, notice, envision)
- Stance calibration section
- Success envisioning section
- Closing "Ready" statement

**Flexible Elements (Adapt to Context):**
- Specific section headers
- Depth of each section
- Order of exploration (follow what emerges)
- Language and metaphors used

**The Balance:**

Structure provides container. Emergence fills container. Don't force rigid template when organic flow is working.
